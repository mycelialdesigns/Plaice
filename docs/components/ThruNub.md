# Thru-Nubs

<img src="/docs/images/thrunubs/thrunubside.jpg" alt="A Thru-nub, Side View" width="256" height="256"> <img src="/docs/images/thrunubs/thrunubtop.jpg" alt="A Thru-nub, Top View" width="256" height="256"> 

A Plaice thru-nub is essentially a clipped and a nubbed [plug](/docs/components/Plugs.md) fused onto the backs of one another, but with the usual M2 interface
replaced by a single thru-hole for a M2 bolt. 

They are used in a couple of different scenarios in Plaice, both starting with a [nubbed connector](/docs/design/ClipInterface.md) inserted into
one half of a Plaice [socket](/docs/design/Sockets.md).

From there, a [beam](/docs/components/Beams.md) may be attached using a thru-nub to cover the gap between the plate [socket](/docs/design/Sockets.md) and the beam's internal
socket with a clipped plug.

Or another Plaice [plate](/docs/components/Plates.md) can be stacked on top of the first one and secured with a clipped plug, yielding a thicker composite plate.
