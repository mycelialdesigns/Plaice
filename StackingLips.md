# Stacking Lips

<img src="/docs/images/stackinglips.jpg" alt="Stacking Lips, Intruding and Protruding" width="256" height="256">

A Plaice stacking lip is a terminal slide connector which can be used to terminate a slide (here assumed to be directed vertically)
with geometry which mates nicely with the chamfered geometry of corners.

Stacking lips may be used to construct stackable boxes out of Plaice components, but they are not generally suitable
for large stacking boxes by themselves, since the deflection of Plaice plates making up the walls may be too high
for stacked boxes to seat properly. Consequently, for larger boxes, it is recommended to use the "thick" variant
of corner stacking caps to ensure that at least the corners of boxes are always supported. (TODO: reference)
