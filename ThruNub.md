# Thru-Nubs

A Plaice thru-nub is essentially a clipped and a nubbed plug fused onto the backs of one another, but with the usual M2 interface
replaced by a single thru-hole for a M2 bolt. 

They are used in a couple of different scenarios in Plaice, both starting with a nubbed connector inserted into
one half of a Plaice socket.

From there, a beam may be attached using a thru-nub to cover the gap between the plate socket and the beam's internal
socket with a clipped plug.

Or another Plaice plate can be stacked on top of the first one and secured with a clipped plug, yielding a thicker composite plate. 

# Printing

The "printable" modification of thru-nubs may be printed without supports.
